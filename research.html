Ongoing Research

Social institutions across Canada have shifted how they operate to adjust to the new reality of lockdowns under the pandemic, impacting service delivery to people experiencing homelessness. In the first 8 months since March 2020, our team interviewed front-line service workers across Canada who work closely with vulnerable people experiencing homelessness. This aims to provide ‘near time’ empirical evidence about how people experiencing homelessness are being policed during the pandemic. This research also asks:

  • What are the needs of people experiencing homelessness during the pandemic and how are they being addressed by social institutions?
  • How were people experiencing homelessness impacted by initial pandemic lockdowns in receiving front-line services?
  • How are police-homeless encounters changing in the pandemic environment?
